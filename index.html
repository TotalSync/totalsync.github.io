About Me

Early Life and Wired Wizards

I was born and raised in Wilmington North Carolina. I have always been interested in circuits from a young age. I used to look at older circuit boards and think about them like tiny cities. Mythbusters was a great help in continuing to grow this interest. Around middle school I began to pursue the idea of becoming an engineer. I attended the science fair on three occasions, and earned an honorable mention at the North Carolina State Science Fair. I began taking apart things to try and figure out how they worked. I was originally fascinated by the mechanical components of machines, however, that changed when I joined my local First Robotics Team: 4534 The Wired Wizards. When I saw the robot for the first time, I understood how it worked mechanically, but I was fascinated by how it could be controlled from a long range. At that point I began to learn, in earnest, the basics of electricity.

I worked with my team over three years during highschool to help design, build, run, and fix our robots over the course of a competition season. The First Robotics Competition (FRC) I participated in were Stronghold, Steamworks, and PowerUp. I learned many things while in my team. I learned how to communicate and work professionally while under the stresses of a competitive environment. In my final year, I was given the opportunity to be the leader of the design team for our entire team. In this position, it was my job ot oversee the design process and later in the build process, to make sure that the parts created were adhering to the design.
